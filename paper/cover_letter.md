# Cover Letter Draft

Dear Editor,

Please consider the manuscript, "Release-Structured, Revision-Aware Real-Time Nowcasting of U.S. GDP," for publication in *Applied Economics*.

The paper studies U.S. GDP nowcasting in a real-time setting where GDP is treated as a sequence of official releases rather than as a single final target. The empirical design distinguishes advance, second, third, and mature GDP releases and evaluates forecasts using release-specific real-time information sets. The model comparison includes autoregressive, bridge, standard dynamic factor, release-structured dynamic factor, and revision-aware dynamic factor specifications.

The main contribution is a disciplined release-structured evaluation of GDP nowcasts. The paper does not claim that the proposed models dominate at every stage. Instead, it shows that structured models are most useful for the second and third GDP releases, while the advance release remains difficult and simple bridge or factor benchmarks remain competitive. This framing is important for applied users because it identifies when release-structure modeling adds value and when conventional nowcasting benchmarks remain hard to beat.

The manuscript also provides robustness checks for pandemic exclusion, pre- and post-global-financial-crisis subsamples, expanded indicator panels, alternative data choices, and small-sample forecast comparison tests. The results support a model-family conclusion rather than an unconditional single-model dominance claim.

The accompanying repository contains a reproducibility note and a frozen result build used as the source of truth for all reported numbers.

Thank you for considering the manuscript.

Sincerely,

Ha Minh
